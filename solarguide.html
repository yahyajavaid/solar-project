Choose the right solar system type based on your energy needs, budget, and grid reliability in your area.

Off-Grid System

Ideal for areas with no or unreliable grid electricity. Complete energy independence with battery storage. Perfect for frequent loadshedding areas.

On-Grid System

Connects to WAPDA, enables net metering. Saves monthly bills by selling excess power back to the grid, but doesn't work during outages.

Hybrid System

Combines both grid connection and batteries. Most flexible option providing backup during outages while enabling net metering benefits.

Understanding different panel technologies helps you make the right choice for your budget and space constraints.

Monocrystalline

High efficiency (18-22%), compact size, excellent for limited roof space. Higher initial cost but better long-term value.

Polycrystalline

Budget-friendly option with good efficiency (15-18%). Slightly larger size, ideal for cost-conscious installations.

Bifacial / N-type

Emerging high-efficiency technology (20-24%) for premium systems. Can generate power from both sides.

üîã 4. Battery Backup (Optional)

If you're planning for outages or off-grid setups, batteries are essential. Choose based on your backup requirements:

  • Days of Backup: Determine how many days you want power without sun (typically 1-3 days)
  • System Voltage: Choose 12V, 24V, or 48V depending on your inverter size and capacity
  • Depth of Discharge (DOD): Affects usable capacity - 80% for lithium, 50% for lead-acid
  • Battery Type: Lithium-ion for longer life (10+ years) or lead-acid for lower upfront cost

Use our Battery Sizing Calculator to determine the perfect battery capacity for your specific needs and backup requirements.

üåê 5. Net Metering in Pakistan

Net metering allows you to export unused solar electricity back to the grid and receive credits on your monthly bill. Here's what you need to know:

  • Application Process: Apply through your local DISCO (IESCO, LESCO, KE, PESCO, etc.)
  • System Size Limits: Usually limited to your sanctioned load or consumption history
  • Billing Mechanism: Monthly net billing with annual true-up
  • Requirements: Bi-directional meter, approved equipment, safety certifications
