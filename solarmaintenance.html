üîß Essential Maintenance Tips

Follow these proven maintenance practices to keep your solar system operating at peak performance in Pakistan's challenging environment.

1
Clean Panels Regularly

Dust, bird droppings, and pollution can reduce efficiency by up to 25% in Pakistani cities. Clean panels every 15-30 days using soft water and a microfiber cloth or squeegee. Avoid harsh chemicals and clean during cooler morning or evening hours.

2
Inspect for Shade Sources

Ensure trees, buildings, or new constructions aren't casting shadows on your panels. Even partial shading on one panel can drastically reduce the output of the entire string. Trim vegetation regularly and monitor for new obstructions.

3
Check for Physical Damage

Look for cracks, hotspots, discoloration, or signs of corrosion. Check for loose mounting hardware after windstorms. Contact a certified solar technician immediately if any damage is detected to prevent further deterioration.

4
Monitor System Performance

Use your inverter display or monitoring app to track daily energy production. Sudden drops in output may signal equipment problems, shading issues, or the need for cleaning. Compare performance to previous months and seasons.

5
Maintain Battery Systems

For off-grid systems with lead-acid batteries, check water levels monthly and keep terminals clean and dry. Monitor battery voltage and temperature. Replace batteries every 5-8 years or as recommended by the manufacturer.

Pakistan Climate Tip: During monsoon season, inspect your system more frequently for water damage, loose connections, and accumulated debris that could affect drainage.

üìã Annual Professional Inspection Checklist

Schedule a professional inspection annually to catch issues before they become expensive problems:

  • Visual inspection of all solar panels for cracks, discoloration, or damage
  • Inverter diagnostic check for error codes, alerts, and performance issues
  • Complete electrical inspection including cables, connectors, and junction boxes
  • Grounding system verification and safety equipment testing
  • Mounting structure inspection for corrosion, looseness, or wear
  • Performance analysis comparing current output to system specifications
  • Battery system comprehensive check (for off-grid installations)
  • Documentation update including warranty and maintenance records

‚ö†Ô∏è When to Call a Professional

Don't attempt DIY repairs on these issues. Contact a certified solar technician immediately if you notice:

  • Sudden or unexplained drop in energy production (>10% decrease)
  • Physical damage including cracks, burns, or hot spots on panels
  • Inverter error messages, warnings, or unexpected shut-offs
  • Battery issues including leaks, swelling, overheating, or voltage problems
  • Electrical problems such as sparking, burning smells, or tripped breakers
  • Storm damage or suspected lightning strikes to your system
  • Animals or pests nesting under or around your solar panels
